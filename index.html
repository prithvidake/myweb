Hi! I am a PhD Student in Chemical Engineering at the University of California, Santa Barbara. I completed my undergraduate education from the Institute of Chemical Technology, Mumbai (formerly UDCT).

I am passionate about mathematical modeling and will like to work in a field that fully engages that aspect. However, I am equally excited to work on an experimental front. I love programming and have extensively worked with Python on optimization and numerical methods in Chemical Engineering. 

More about me

“Give a man a fish and he will eat for a day. Teach a man to fish and he will eat for a lifetime” - Lao Tzu


What You See Is What You Mean (WYSIWYM) is an interesting concept in LATEX. In my life, it has
been What You See Is What You Try. The interplay between the complexity of physical happenings and the humbling effort to describe it with a set of equations resonates deeply with me as an engineer. I am particularly interested in process control, mathematical modelling and computational research.

I have recently completed my Bachelors in Chemical Engineering (B.Chem.Engg) from ICT Mumbai and would be pursuing my graduate studies at University of California, Santa Barbara working towards a PhD in ChE!

Throughout my undergraduation, I have tried to work on as many modelling and computational problems as possible. It has been a lot fun!
